Integrals measure how much a quantity accumulates. At first, the concepts may appear unrelated. The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (FTC) reveals the connection by showing that the derivative of an integral function equals the original integrand and that definite integrals can be computed through antiderivatives. 5 Imagine water flowing into a tank at a steady rate. An integral measures the accumulation of flow rate over time and gives the total amount of water in the tank. If the derivative of that accumulation function is taken, the result is the flow rate again. In other words, the derivative shows how fast the total is growing at each moment, matching the original inflow rate. 6 In this lesson, the inverse relationship between derivatives and integrals is
